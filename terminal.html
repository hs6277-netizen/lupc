Our services

Liquid Bulk

In the heart of Europe’s leading port and industrial complex, customers can rely on our flexible and safe infrastructure. We are constantly expanding our unique platform of best-in-class liquid bulk terminals, including the latest state-of-the-art liquid bulk terminals in North West Europe. 

Dry Bulk

In the heart of Europe’s leading port and industrial complex, customers can rely on our flexible and safe infrastructure. We are constantly expanding our unique platform of best-in-class dry bulk terminals, so that we can maintain our position as market leader in terms of large-scale, open air storage with a flexible port infrastructure. We are substantially investing in our covered storage capacity as well, to accommodate products as agricultural products, biomass and building materials.

Break Bulk

Fit-for-purpose storage and handling solutions for overweight and over-dimensional project cargo are available at several locations in Europe’s major ports. These types of breakbulk cargo include steel coils, components for offshore windmills and other kinds of heavy project cargo. It is our aim to increase our breakbulk activities in Europe.

Logistics services

At several locations, logistics services are offered that allow our customers to further optimize their value chain. Some examples are freight forwarding, shipping agencies and transportation services.

Be aware of spoofing

Fraud and Scam Alert

We have become aware of fraudulent business propositions by parties who refer to Hes international Europe group companies and/or its terminals, employees, names and logos. These fraudulent activities are commonly also referred to as ‘Storage Spoofing’, which is an umbrella term to describe all activities involving the sale of non-existent storage capacity and non-existent stocks of product at storage terminals.

Read more

The targets for this type of fraud are national and multinational companies that either operate or are looking for storage facilities in port areas, as well as potential buyers of the products stored at these terminals. The products and storage capacity are offered under false pretences and do not actually exist.In general, this type of business fraud is aimed at obtaining payment or at obtaining invoices, (counter) signed documents and/or email confirmations that could be used in fraudulent business propositions to other targets.    

The Dutch National Cyber Security Centre has made available a list of websites that are implicated in Storage Spoofing. For more information, please visit: https://ferm-rotterdam.nl/blacklist.

Warning signs

  • email correspondence through free web-based email accounts such as Gmail, Yahoo, Hotmail, Live, etc;
  • unsolicited offers, often involving discounts or bonuses;
  • requests for up-front money payments;
  • requests for personal and financial information, often at an early stage;
  • an insistence on urgency;
  • business propositions relating to a short storage term;
  • poor use of English in communications.
